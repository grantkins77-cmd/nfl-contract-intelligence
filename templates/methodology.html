How the Scoring Works

Every player receives a Value Score from 0 to 100 that answers one question: is this player outperforming or underperforming their contract relative to peers at the same position? Here's exactly how it's calculated.

1

Performance Score

Each position uses a custom formula built around the stats most predictive of winning. All scores are multiplied by a games-played weight (games played ÷ 17, capped at 1.0) to account for players who missed significant time.

QB
Passing EPA + (CPOE × 10)

EPA captures efficiency on every throw. CPOE measures whether a QB completes passes above or below the difficulty of his attempts.

WR
Receiving EPA + (WOPR × 50)

EPA rewards high-value catches. WOPR (Weighted Opportunity Rating) combines target share and air yards share to measure route involvement.

RB
(Rush EPA + Rec EPA) × 0.3
+ (YPC − 4.0) × 10 × 0.4
+ (Rush Yards ÷ 10) × 0.3

RBs are scored on three equally important dimensions: efficiency (EPA), yards per carry above league average (4.0), and raw volume.

TE
Receiving EPA + (Target Share × 100)

EPA captures play quality. Target share rewards TEs who are featured in the passing game rather than merely serviceable blockers.

2

Normalization (0 – 100)

Raw performance scores are normalized within each position group for the 2025 season. The best performer at each position scores 100, the worst scores 0, and everyone else falls proportionally in between. This makes scores comparable across positions and seasons.

A performance score of 72 means this player performed better than 72% of qualified players at their position in 2025 — regardless of how that compares to other positions.
3

Linear Regression vs. Salary

Within each position group, we run a linear regression of performance score against annual contract value (APY). This establishes what level of performance is expected for a given salary.

A player earning $30M/yr has a higher performance bar than a player earning $5M/yr. The regression captures this relationship across all players at the position.

Expected Performance = what the regression predicts for a player at their salary tier.
Value Delta = Actual Performance − Expected Performance.
4

Value Score

The Value Delta (regression residual) is normalized to a 0–100 scale within each position, producing the final Value Score.

Elite Value Score ≥ 75 · Top 25% at position — significantly outperforming contract
Fair Value Score 25–75 · Middle 50% — performing near salary expectations
Overpaid Score < 25 · Bottom 25% — underperforming relative to contract
A score of 50 means the player is performing exactly at what their salary predicts. Above 50 = contract bargain. Below 50 = overpaid.

Stat Glossary

EPA
Expected Points Added. Measures how much a play increased or decreased the team's expected points, accounting for down, distance, and field position. The single most predictive metric in NFL analytics.
CPOE
Completion Percentage Over Expected. Compares a QB's actual completion rate to a model-predicted rate based on the difficulty of each throw (air yards, receiver separation, pressure). Removes the bias from playing with elite receivers.
WOPR
Weighted Opportunity Rating. A composite of target share (40%) and air yards share (60%) that measures how much of the passing game flows through a receiver. Highly correlated with future receiving production.
YPC
Yards Per Carry. The league average is approximately 4.0 yards per carry. The RB formula explicitly rewards carries above this threshold, treating average rushing efficiency as the baseline.
Target Share
Percentage of a team's total pass attempts directed at a given receiver. Used for TEs where absolute receiving totals can be misleading — a TE on a run-heavy team may have fewer targets but a high share of available opportunities.
APY
Average Per Year contract value (in millions). The primary salary variable used in the regression. Does not account for guaranteed money or restructured contracts.

Important Caveats

  • Rookie contracts are labeled separately because players on pre-extension rookie deals earn far below market rate by design. Comparing them directly to veterans would make every rookie appear as an "Elite Value" contract.
  • Only 2025 regular season stats are used. Playoff performance, injuries to supporting cast (offensive line, quarterback), and team scheme are not factored in.
  • Position minimums apply. Players with fewer than 100 pass attempts (QB), 20 carries (RB), 20 targets (WR), or 15 targets (TE) are excluded to avoid noise from small samples.
  • Scores are relative, not absolute. A value score of 70 means outperforming 70% of peers — not that the player is objectively good. A bad position class lifts all scores.
